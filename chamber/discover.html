Welcome to Lima!

CULTURAL HERITAGE OF HUMANITY

The Historic Center of Lima includes the Plaza Mayor of Lima, the Cathedral of Lima, the Archbishop's Palace of Lima, the Church of Santo Domingo, the convent of San Francisco, among many other tourist attractions. Thanks to this, in 1991 the Historic Center of Lima was declared Cultural Heritage of Humanity by UNESCO.

THE ONLY SOUTH AMERICAN CAPITAL WITH A SEA VIEW

The geographical location of Lima is unique in the region, since there is no other South American capital that has access to the sea. To better take advantage of this enormous potential, the Costa Verde was built in the 1970s, linking the beaches of the capital's coastal districts and making them accessible to all residents. Thanks to this, Lima residents can freely visit spas and practice water sports such as surfing or swimming and even fishing.
